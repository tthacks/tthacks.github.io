Book 1: Blacksby Nickel and the Starlit Path

Chapter 3

The caravan traveled out of the town and through farmlands and mines in the valley, before finally summiting the edge of the valley and heading into spirit lands. Normally, human travelers venturing from the safety of their homes would be set upon by angry spirits, but the caravan carried the insignia of the Eldersbrook, representing the treaty between the titans and the humans. No small spirit would be messing with them on their journey, and risk punishment by the titans.

Blacksby stayed in his personal room, reading books about Yi Song, the first titan they would face. Yi Song was a spirit in the shape of a bear, and he valued bravery above all. He lived in a grand palace north of Wolfordshire, in a treacherous range of mountains called the Devil’s teeth. The path there alone was treacherous and winding, and while the book did not give details about the trial itself (those who passed through the trials were forbidden to speak about their experiences - Blacksby himself did not know what his father faced), he was sure that half of the battle was not chickening out on the road there.

Late at night, they finally decided to set up camp alongside the wooded forest. While the servants entertained themselves and Blacksby, telling stories along the fireside and drawing constellations in the sky, Reylin sat alone in his tent, reading over maps for the next day’s journey. Melie knocked her hand on the tent door and poked her head in.

“Sir, I’m sorry if I’m interrupting something,” she said, sheepishly. Reylin folded up his spectacles and maps in the slow, careful manner he did everything. “I just wanted to thank you for letting me come along.”

He nodded, simply. “Please, young miss Melie. Come in. I can tell you have lots of questions.”

The young girl closed the tent flap behind her and took a seat across from Reylin. He began pouring her a cup of tea.

“Sir, you’ve chased me off from the courtyard lots of times. I know you take this trials thing very seriously. So I was wondering…”

“Why I let you come along?”

She took a sip of her tea. “Yes. Exactly.”

Reylin leaned back for a moment, thinking of what to tell her. “What do you know, about the history of the Trials?” he asked.

She shook her head. “Very little. I never could pay much attention in history class.”

“There was a time long go, before the foundation of the great families, where man did not live in the villages and valleys we inhabit now. Humans lived scattered across the landscape, intermingled with the spirits in the wilds. It was a time of uncertainty and distrust, for the spirits who owned the land were powerful, and would hunt the humans for sport.

“The humans knew that they were no match for the spirits, and going to war to fight them for their land would be a fruitless endeavor that would only result in the extinction of humankind. But one family realized that while they would not be able to defeat the spirits by force, there might be a peaceful way to strike up a balance between the two. This family, as we would come to know them as the Great Family of Golden, gathered some other families he knew across the land to form a plan. Together with eight other families of repute, they faced the Council of the Five Titans, the greatest spirits who held the domain. They asked that humans be allowed to settle in certain lands in peace, where they and the spirits would be able to live without getting in each other's way.

One of the great spirits, Ool Olang, wanted to know how or why this would possibly benefit them. The great fa families had planned for this. They believed that spirits only taunted them because they believed humans to be inferior to them. Ool Olang is known for his wisdom, so the humans argued that the humans were wise and worthy of their respect, and capable of solving any puzzle the spirits could throw at them.

"They bargained with the spirits, arguing the humans' ability to excel in all that which the great titans valued, and therefore were worthy of having their own lands. Wisdom for Ool Olang, strategy for Kal Alli, strength for Gyar Ada, integrity for Wei Ha, bravery for Yi Song. The spirits were intrigued, and created the Trials of Eldersbrook, and said that if humans could pass their trials, then they would be granted land. The eight families each chose a member from their family to complete the trials. And the spirits, staying true to their word, created the valleys in which humans could live and not be disturbed by spirits. This agreement came to be known as the Treaty of Eldersbrook. But the spirits, believing this to simply be a fluke, insist upon retesting each of the great families with each new generation, waiting for the day where peace will make the humans grow complacent, greedy, and soft. It has been six generations since the first Trials of Eldersbrook, and so far, none of the great families have failed. But Blacksby's situation is unique. He has no siblings, and the Nickel family has already seen much tragedy. If he fails, the great spirit Gyar Ada has sworn to wipe out the valley from which the Nickel family hails."

Melie let out a little gasp. "But why? Wouldn’t that break the contract?"

"Gyar Ada never wanted to make a treaty with the humans. He was outvoted, and as the titan of strength, he always looks for ways to prove that he cannot be controlled by the other titans. The Wolfordshire Valley was created on lands originally owned by him. Gyar Ada has been waiting for the opportunity to take his land back, and the rest of the spirit masters believe that this might be his opportunity. Blacksby has no siblings, and no other great families live in the valley. He alone can be the one to represent them, and if he fails, Gyar Ada will not hesitate to destroy the humans and the Wolfordshire Valley, because then he will be free from his contract."

Melie felt her bones tremble at the thought of her whole valley being destroyed. She had known about the Trials her whole life, but never had understood the importance of them. She'd just assumed that it was a vanity project, a familial obligation, not an obligation to every life that she'd ever met. She regretted everything she'd ever said to Blacksby about the matter.

"And does Blacksby know all of this?" she asked. The shadows from the campfire outside the tent gave a sudden jolt, and Melie jumped as if she thought they were in an earthquake.

"Young master Nickel knows the legend of the great titans, and he knows that passing the tests is important," Master Reylin explained. He reached out to the teapot to pour another cup of steaming tea. "But he has not yet met the Titans, and he does not know of Gyar Ada's plans. The stakes are already high enough, and to burden him with the information, with no power to back out or give up... I do not think it would help him succeed. It would only make him suffer."

He refilled Melie's mug with steaming tea as well, and she hugged it close to her for warmth, but did not drink it.

"This is why I let you come along," he said, "Blacksby may be responsible for so many lives, but I do not think he can be responsible for them alone. He needs you, Melie," he explained, in the flickering, fading light of the tent, "To remind him, in the darkness, what light looks like."
