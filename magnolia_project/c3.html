Chapter 3

Who would have thought that Kirk would be the first of us to have a date? In fact, since that first Chemistry class, I haven’t seen him apart from that Christie girl. They’ve walked to classes together, eaten lunch together… sitting at Pepperbelly Creamery, this is the first time I’ve actually seen him alone. Instead of staring at his phone today, he’s staring dreamily at the ceiling like they’ve posted a picture of her up there. “Man, Christie is just amazing. She’s so funny, and smart, and her hair smells like mandarin oranges. Why don’t they make men’s shampoo smell so good?”

I considered making a snappy remark about how women’s and men’s shampoo work the same and it’s all about preference and branding, but I don’t think he would get it. “I don’t know, Kirk. I guess it’s just one of life’s little mysteries.”

“Of course, it’s not all about me. How’s your new lab partner? That Mitchell guy?”

“Michael,” I corrected him, and then immediately dismissed the subject. “He’s fine.” Actually, I don’t want to get into the subject of my new lab partner. After asking me point-blank if my father burned down a building, he only got stranger. He didn’t have any friends that I knew of. We had a fire drill two days ago, and he spent the whole time lying in the grass, laughing, like he’d set the fire himself. He refused to go back inside. He looks at the world with wide eyes, as if he’s seeing everything for the very first time. Like he just stepped off a spaceship. Or maybe he’s stoned?

“That’s good. I feel totally bad for abandoning you, but I’m glad it’s all working out,” immediately, his phone lit up—a text message. He dropped everything to pick it up and type a response. I sighed and slumped in my seat to look out the window, “By the way, Christie’s coming by in like, thirty seconds,” Kirk threw his phone to the side. I immediately straightened up.

“What?” The last thing I wanted to see was Lovey-Dovey Kirk—somehow, it was more infuriating than Conspiracy Kirk. Besides, the point of getting ice cream every week was to unwind, and I knew this was just going to set me on edge.

“Yeah, I invited her. It’s cool, right?” he asked, and slid to the end of the booth. No, it’s not cool at all, I thought, but the front bell was already jingling behind me. Without even turning around, I knew it was her by the way Kirk’s face changed. He immediately stood up and ran his fingers through his orange hair to ensure the most shocking appearance possible. Christie was dressed in Pepto-Bismol pink with a matching beret planted on her bushy hair. She spotted him and scuttled over to our booth, planting an enthusiastic kiss on his cheek.

“Christie, you know Augustine, of course,” he said, gesturing to me. I still sat in my booth, trying not to scowl. The wave of mandarin-orange scent hit my nose like a wall. She didn’t just use orange shampoo, she used orange conditioner and orange perfume and possibly even orange hair-spray. I made a mental note to check my shampoo later to make sure I never smell as revoltingly sweet as she did. I nodded hello and she took a seat beside Kirk. They squeezed in so close to each other, you would think the edges of the booth were lava. Kirk was the first to talk. “I was hoping you two could get to know each other.”

"What’s to know?" I said curtly, not even looking at Christie. I was mad at Kirk for inviting her without asking me, but what’s new? Christie’s big eyes pop at the sight of Kirk’s Belly Breaker split.

“That’s the most ice cream I’ve ever seen in my life!” she gasped, “Are you really going to eat all of it?”

“I might need your help,” Kirk said, taking a spoonful and feeding it to Christie. I shuddered and rolled my eyes.

“You seemed to do just fine on the first thirty-two,” I said, referring to his record, “And you’ll be disqualified if you share.”

“It’s fine. I have a big enough lead for now. No one will catch up to me,” he said. This is the same kid who forced me to come with him every week in the middle of the winter just to watch him eat one of these dumb things. It was the coldest winter on record, but he was afraid his competition would try and get an edge on him.

Christie batted her eyes and swallows the ice cream, “So Augustine, I just wanted to say, I’m really impressed with your writing. Your articles on the website are really good!”

“You told her?” I stabbed my spoon into my ice cream and it stood straight up. “I said you couldn’t tell anyone else I helped you with that dumb website!”

“I was showing her the writing, and I didn’t want to take credit for your work!” Kirk rebuked.

“If I cared about the credit, I wouldn’t have written it anonymously.”

“Well I hope you don’t think about quitting, because we’re about to get the big break we’ve been waiting for!” Kirk queues up another spoonful for his girlfriend.

“What big break?”

“The head writer for BlackoutBunker.net said he’s really interested in our work. He might take us on as part of his team. He’s super mysterious, but he has thousands of readers every day. People really respect him. Don’t you want to be a part of that?” Kirk spun his spoon around and around between his fingers.

“No. No way. I’m not working with that weirdo,” I said.

“Come on, Augustine. Ever since your father was arrested, you’re being insufferable. What’s gotten into you?”

I pushed my ice cream away. Insufferable? He’s being insufferable—I have better things to do than peddle crap. For once, I’m going to work on something real, something people care about. “I’m going to prove my father’s innocence. And write about it. On the blog, for everyone to see.”

With that, I stood up from my chair and toss my ice cream cup into the trash. “You two probably want some alone time on your date,” I hissed. Before I could let Kirk argue, I made a beeline for the door. Ever since he started dating that helium-voice airhead, he’s turned into someone I hardly recognize.
